Step-by-Step Instructions: Shower Study

Data Button - Shower Button - Searching Data Files - Selecting Data - Analyzing
Shower Candidates - View Plot Controls - Change Caption - The Plot - Save Plot - Done

Keep two windows open, one with these instructions and the other with the shower study web pages.
Go back and forth between them.
Use the scroll bar to move the instructions.
Look for the orange highlights for guidance.

We will create a plot with data from the QuarkNet Traveling Array dated 10/29/2006 to 10/30/2006. QuarkNet Traveling Array has been entered into the e-Lab as a school even though it does not seem like your typical school

Step 1:Click on the Data button in the main menu.
Step 2:Click on the Shower button in the submenu.
Step 3.1:Look at the search page. On the right is a button to run the study and links to a tutorial, step-by-step instructions, FAQs and related milestones. The legend shows icons for information about each data file.
Step 3.2:We want to find all data for the QuarkNet Traveling Array for Oct. 29-30, 2006. We decided on these dates because there was overlap in the data during this period.
a) Select School in the pulldown list
b) Enter QuarkNet Traveling Array in the search field.
c) Click on the little arrow next to "Advanced Search."
Step 3.3:a) Select Start Date in the pulldown list for dates and enter "10/29/2006" in the first date field and "10/30/2006" in the second date field. You can also use the calendar.
b) Click Search Data
Step 3.4:a) Click on the little arrow next to "QuarkNet Traveling Array" to see the data files.
Step 3.5:a) Click on the little arrow next to "October 2006" to see the data files for that month.
Step 3.6:Notice there are data for two detectors and that they are all in an array configuration. See the data by clicking on the date and additional information by clicking on the comments bubble.
Step 4:a) Click all the checkboxes associated with detector 5078 and 5080 except the rightmost. It does not have enough data.
Step 4.1: a) Then click Run shower study on the right.
Step 4.2:Look at the display of all the data files you have selected. You can view the raw data, check the statistics, and compare them by using the links on the right. Also check that the geometry files are there. You cannot do a shower study without them.
Step 5:To understand the input parameters, you can click on the question mark next to each.
a) Set the center of the detectors to the detector id 5080
b) the event gate to 12 ns
c) the Detector Coincidence to 2
d) the Channel Coincidence to 3
e) the Event Coincidence to 2
f) Click Analyze. We will run the analysis on our local computer. This will take awhile so don't be alarmed if you do not get a plot right away.
Step 5.1:If your analysis takes very little time to run, you may get your plot right away. More often, you get a progress bar displaying how the analysis is doing. You can click on Queue study to allow the analysis to go on in the background. You can also click on Analysis output to see the inner workings. This might help you if you encounter an error in your analysis.
Step 5.2:You can now click on Analyses on the navigation bar or analysis list in the text to see the progress of you job.
Step 5.3:When your job is done, you will see an entry on the Analysis List like this. You can click on the link and see the plot.
For the shower study there are actually two links, one for the Shower Study and the other when you generate each Event Plot. You will encounter these later.
Step 6:Here are a list of the possible shower candidates. In this case there is only one candidate. The plot, highlighted in blue, is on the right. If there were more than one, you could click on the event date and display the plot associated with each event. The data table shows all the events associated with each candidate.
a) Click on the plot on the right to see a bigger version. Notice that the caption, all those words at the top, is really too large.
b) Click on Change at the bottom to go back and fix caption in the plot parameters.
Step 7:a) To change the caption on the plot, you will need to use the plot controls.
Click on the small arrow to view the Plot Controls.
Step 8:a) Change the z-max to 16 and edit the list of data files from the figure caption and replace it with something simpler such as
"Data: QuarkNet Traveling Array
Oct 29-30, 2006
Detectors: 5078, 5080
Keep the other settings
b) Consider executing the analysis on the grid or the I2U2 Cluster.
c) ClickAnalyze.
New caption
Step 9:a) Click on the small plot to open a window with this plot.
b) Scroll down in the plot window past the date so you can save the plot permanently.
Step 10:Enter a name for the plot similar, but not the same as the one shown and click Save Plot. If you do this tutorial more than once, choose another name for the plot.
You will have access to this plot for your poster.
Step 11:Note you have successfully saved your plot and click Close.
Also close the window with the plot. You can do more plots for other shower candidates or go back to choosing a new data set by clicking Shower in the menu. Anytime you can click View Plots to see the plots you have made.
