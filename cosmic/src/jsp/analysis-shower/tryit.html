Step-by-Step Instructions: Shower Study

Data Button - Shower Button - Searching Data Files - Selecting Data - Analyzing
Shower Candidates - View Plot Controls - Change Caption - The Plot - Save Plot - Done

  • You can make a plot by hand of frequency (y variable) vs. the multiplicity (x variable).
  • This can be related to the number of muons but depends on geometry (2 stacked counters count 1 muon, whereas 2 horizontally separated counters count 2 muons).
  • Keep two windows open, one with these instructions and the other with the shower study web pages.
  • Go back and forth between them.
  • Use the scroll bar to move the instructions.
  • Look for the blue highlights for guidance.

We will create a plot with data from the QuarkNet Traveling Array dated 10/29/2006 to 10/30/2006. QuarkNet Traveling Array has been entered into the e-Lab as a school even though it does not seem like your typical school

Step 1:Click on the Data button in the main menu.
Step 2:Click on the Shower button in the submenu.
Step 3.1:Look at the search page. On the right, there are links to a tutorial, step-by-step instructions, FAQs and related milestones. The legend shows icons for information about each data file.
Step 3.2:We want to find all data for the Fermilab Test Array for Sep. 1 and 2, 2016. We had two detectors in the same room collecting data simultaneously.
a) Select School in the pulldown list
b) Enter Fermilab Test Array in the search field.
c) Click on the little arrow next to "Advanced Search."
Step 3.3:a) Select Start Date in the pulldown list for dates and enter "09/01/2016" in the first date field and "09/07/2016" in the second date field. You can also use the calendar.
b) Choose Blessed: All. We shall use the reliable data from both detectors, but they have not yet passed the "Blessed" categorization process..
c) Click Search Data.
Step 3.4:a) Click on the little arrow next to "Fermilab Test Array" to see the data files.
Step 3.5:a) Click on the little arrow next to "September 2016" to see the data files for that month.
Step 3.6:Notice there are data for two detectors and that they are both in an array configuration. See the data by clicking on the date and additional information by clicking on the comments bubble.
Step 4:a) Click all the checkboxes associated with detector 6119 and 6148. You can also use the "All" option.
Step 4.1: a) Then click Run shower study on the right.
Step 4.2:Look at the display of all the data files you have selected. You can view the raw data, check the statistics, and compare them by using the links on the right. Also check that the geometry files are there. You cannot do a shower study without them.
Step 5:To understand the input parameters, you can click on the question mark next to each.
a) Set the center of the detectors to the detector id 6119
b) the event gate to 1000 ns
c) the Detector Coincidence to 2
d) the Channel Coincidence to 2
e) the Event Coincidence to 2
f) Click Analyze. We will run the analysis on our local computer. This will take awhile so don't be alarmed if you do not get a plot right away.
Step 5.1:If your analysis takes very little time to run, you may get your plot right away. More often, you get a progress bar displaying how the analysis is doing. You can click on Queue study to allow the analysis to go on in the background. You can also click on Analysis output to see the inner workings. This might help you if you encounter an error in your analysis.
Step 5.2:You can now click on Analyses on the navigation bar or analysis list in the text to see the progress of you job.
Step 5.3:When your job is done, you will see an entry on the Analysis List like this. You can click on the link and see the plot.
For the shower study there are actually two links, one for the Shower Study and the other when you generate each Event Plot. You will encounter these later.
Step 6:Here are a list of the possible shower candidates. The plot is on the right. You can click on the event date and display the plot associated with each event. The data table shows all the events associated with each candidate.
a) Choose the candidate with event date Sep 1, 2016 01:02:17.
b) Click on the plot on the right to see a bigger version. Notice that the caption, all those words at the top, is really too messy.
c) Click on Change at the bottom to go back and fix caption in the plot parameters.
Step 7:a) To change the caption on the plot, you will need to use the plot controls.
Click on the small arrow to view the Plot Controls.
Step 8: a) Change the z-max to 70. This will eliminate extra later hits from 6119 counters.
b) Edit the list of data files from the figure caption and replace it with something simpler such as:
Data: Fermilab Test Array
Sep 01-02, 2016
Detectors: 6119, 6148
Keep the other settings
c) ClickAnalyze.
New caption
Step 9:a) Click on the small plot to open a window with this plot.
b) Scroll down in the plot window past the date so you can save the plot permanently.
Step 10:Enter a name for the plot similar, but not the same as the one shown and click Save Plot. If you do this tutorial more than once, choose another name for the plot.
You will have access to this plot for your poster.
Step 11:Note you have successfully saved your plot and click Close.
Also close the window with the plot. You can do more plots for other shower candidates or go back to choosing a new data set by clicking Shower in the menu. Anytime you can click View Plots to see the plots you have made.
Step 12: This step onward is optional.
a) Click on the box by "View Multiplicity Totals."
b) A new column appears listing the total number of counters hit in the event. This can be less than the number of hits.
c) With the pulldown menu under "Multiplicity Totals," you can select only events with a specific number of counters hit (multiplicity). The default is "All."
d) Notice there are 1376 candidates.
Step 13: Multiplicity 6.
a) Select "6" in the pulldown menu.
b) Now, only 171 candidates with exactly 6 hit counters are listed.
Notice that events come in three varieties in hit counters in DAQs 6119 and 6148:
c) (4,2)
d) (3,3)
e) (2.4)
