Glossary_DAQ DAQ: QuarkNet Data Acquisition Board - circuit board with the CPLD, TMC, microcontroller, and input/output ports mounted. See: Electronic Simulation - shower study data aquisition.
Glossary_GPS Global Positioning System -- A set of 24 satellites primarily providing navigation information. Also, the system distributes coordinated time signals derived from atomic clocks managed by the USNO. Tutorial: GPS
Glossary_UTC Time kept on the Greenwich meridian (longitude zero), five hours ahead of Eastern Standard Time. Times given in UT are almost always given in terms of a 24-hour clock. Thus, 14:42 (often written simply 1442) is 2:42 p.m., and 21:17 (2117) is 9:17 p.m. USNO
Glossary_altitude Altitude is the vertical elevation distance measured from mean sea level. Measure altitude by:
a) Elevation contours from a topographic map: see MapServer for online topo maps.
b) Instrument measurement using a barometer: see Barometer for online instructions.
c) Find an elevation of a nearby landmark: see Elevation for online location selection.
d) Use a survey quality GPS unit. Inexpensive hand-held units will approximate altitude to within only
100 ft.
Glossary_barometric_pressure The pressure force caused by the weight of air above the surface of the Earth. Usually measured by a barometer and given in units of kPa, inches of Hg, or millibars. See "Convert Barometric Pressure" - to learn more.
Glossary_cosmic_ray particle from the cosmos studied by scientists
Glossary_counter Each cosmic ray detector has up to four counters. Here we have two counters, each consisting of a scintillator and photomultiplier tube connected to the DAQ board.
Glossary_decay A process in which an original particle (or nucleus) disappears and is replaced by two or more particles (called decay products.)

Decays conserve energy, charge and momentum. The summed mass of the decay products is less than the mass of the original. The vector sum of the decay products' momentum must equal the momentum of the original particle. The summed charge of all decay products must equal the charge of the original.
Glossary_detector The QuarkNet cosmic ray detector includes counters, DAQ board, power supply, GPS receiver, and cables. Each cosmic ray detector has up to four counters. Here we have two counters, each consisting of a scintillator and photomultiplier tube connected to the DAQ board.
Glossary_flux Rate particles hit. The number of particles per meter2 per time (60 seconds).
Glossary_geometry Arrangement of scintillators - stacked are good for lifetime studies; unstacked are for shower studies.
Glossary_grid A collection of web connected computers for sharing power and storage resources. Grid Cafe - learn more about the Grid.

Glossary_lifetime The mean time for a particle to decay. Similar to half-life for radioactive nuclei.
Glossary_light_leaks The QuarkNet detectors require a dark environment. Plastic or glass scintillator is mated using optical glue and shaped fittings to PMTs. The scintillators are covered with reflective material (aluminum foil works) and then with black paper and tape to make them "light-tight."
Glossary_muon A charged, unstable lepton about 200 times more massive than an electron.
Glossary_photomultiplier_tube Vacuum tube that converts extremely low light levels into electrical signals. Sometimes called a PMT.
Glossary_pulse The pulse width, called "time over threshold", is the time the signal is over the threshold value.
Glossary_pulse_width The pulse width, also called time over threshold, is the total time the signal is over the threshold value.
Glossary_rate The ratio of the number of events during a given length of time: rate = #events / time. Will yield flux when rate is divided by the area of a detector.
Glossary_scintillation_light The light created by a charged particle moving through scintillating plastic.
Glossary_scintillator A material that gives off light when a charged particle passes through it.

These are typically made of plastic that has been doped with molecules that gain energy from the passing electric field of charged particles. The energy is stored in electron orbitals and then promptly released in the form of light.
Glossary_shower The "primary" cosmic rays, which are mainly protons, interact with nucleons in the earth's upper atmosphere. When these "primary" particles interact with nucleons in the atmosphere, they produce mainly pions and kaons, called "secondary" particles. If these "secondary" particles have sufficient energy, they may in turn interact with other particles in the atmosphere producing a "shower" of "secondary" particles. The particles in this shower will strike a wide area on the earth's surface (perhaps several m2 or even km 2 nearly simultaneously. (From the CR Detectors User's Manual)
Glossary_solar_activity The Sun provides a constant flux of charged particles. The level of these particles rise and fall depending on the solar activity. These events result from solar flares. When the particles reach the Earth they cause aurora, geomagnetic storms, and disruption of made-made infrastructure such as navigation systems and electrical power grids. To learn more go to the Space Weather Primer. To see current solar conditions go to the Today's Space Weather.
Glossary_time_dilation The relativistic effect explained by Einstein's Theory that slows the clock of a moving object relative to a stationary observer. Time Dilation Tutorial
Glossary_time_of_day Time of day normally refers to the clock everyone uses. This time is referenced to the Sun and called Solar Time. However, another standard time reference uses the stars instead of the Sun. This is called Sideral Time.
Glossary_time_stamp A coordinated time value attached to a cosmic ray scintillatiion count. The time derives its accuracy from the GPS time signal picked up by the LeadTek GPS module attached to the DAQ. The time precision is of the order of tens of nanoseconds, and this precision allows the coordination of data during a cosmic ray shower.
