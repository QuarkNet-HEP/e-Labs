Altitude is the vertical elevation distance measured from mean sea level. Find altitude by:
a) Elevation contours from a topographic map: See topo maps from National Geographic.
b) Using a pressure altimeter (an instrument that uses a barometer): See Barometer from TED-Ed.
c) Finding elevation of a nearby landmark: See USGS's Geographic Names Information System for online location selection.
d) Using a survey quality GPS unit. Inexpensive hand-held units will approximate altitude to within only
100 ft.
