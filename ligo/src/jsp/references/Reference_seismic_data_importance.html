Milestone: Explain why LIGO monitors the environment at the observatories.

Scientists must be sure they are measuring what they indend. Other processes can mimic the signal and mix with the data. They must separate real data from backgroud noise.

LIGO scientists want to detect gravitational waves, energeic ripples of space, that reach Earth as tiny waves.

However, ground vibrations, seismic waves generated by earthquakes, hurricanes, ocean waves, falling trees or even rabbits jumping, can jiggle the LIGO interferometer mirrors.

This movement masks the effect of a true gravitational wave. Scientists record competing vibrations on other instruments so they can check if a suspected gravitational wave coincides with noise.
