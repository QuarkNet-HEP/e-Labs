Milestone: Look for patterns in the LIGO seismic data.

Research proposals center on a single, well-designed research question that can be answered by collecting and analyzing data.

Make a number of practice plots with Bluestone.

How did the ground shake at LIGO on your birthday?
What does the data look like for a whole week?
What does the data look like on the same day for several years?

(The tutorial will show you how to create a plot.)

As you make more plots, look for interesting features of the data. You can save plots in your logbook. Eventually you'll need to pick a feature as the focus of your research.

The LIGO Hanford Observatory runs five seismometers in different locations along the beam tubes. You need to know these locations and how they are labeled to analyze the data.

Devise a scheme in your logbook to keep track of the seismometer labels.
