Milestone: Explain why LIGO studies seismic data

LIGO's suspended mirrors must be so well shielded from vibration that the random motion of the atoms within the mirrors and suspension fibers can be detected. Ground vibrations under LIGO's detectors must be filtered so completely that the detectors can sense movements of the mirrors at a level of 10-18 meters. LIGO uses seismometers to study ground vibrations to better understand how variations in seismic noise can affect the project's gravitational wave detectors.
