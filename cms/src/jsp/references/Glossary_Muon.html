Muon

The muon (from the Greek letter mu (μ or μ-) used to represent it) is an elementary particle similar to the electron, with negative electric charge and a spin of 1Ú2. Together with the electron, the tauon, and the three neutrinos, it is classified as a lepton. An antimuon (μ+) has positive charge. The combination of two muons is called a dimuon. The Z boson can decays into dimuons. Physicsts can determine the mass of the Z boson by measuring the mass of the resultant dimuons.
