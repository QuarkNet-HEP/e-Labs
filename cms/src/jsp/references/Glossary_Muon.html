Muon

The muon (from the Greek letter μ) is an elementary particle similar to but much more massive than the electron. The muon has electric charge -1 and a spin 1/2; an antimuon is the same but with charge +1. The muon is classified, with the electron, the tau, and the neutrinos, as a lepton. Leptons are fundamental particles that do not interact via the strong force.
