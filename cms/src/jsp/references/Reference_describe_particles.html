Milestone: Describe the particles interacting in the CMS detector.

Scientists often explain the behavior of complex systems in terms of the interactions among less complex parts.


Standard Model chart		 What cast of particle characters do we need for CMS investigations? Some particles are fundamental; others are made of simpler parts. Protons interact in the primary collision; other interactions take place in the time between the primary collision and direct detection in CMS.

In your logbook:

  • Discuss the properties of the proton and how those determine the primary interactions in the LHC beam pipe.
  • Describe the ways that particles not directly observed can still be detected indirectly in CMS data.
