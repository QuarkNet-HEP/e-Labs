Milestone: Describe the particles interacting in the CMS detector.

Scientists often explain the behavior of complex systems in terms of the interactions among less complex parts.

The protons colliding in the LHC are made up of simpler parts, and so are some of the particles interacting with the CMS detector. Other interactions take place in the time between the primary collision and these direct detections. To appreciate the events produced in the LHC and detected by the CMS detector, you've got to know the cast of characters: the fundamental particles, and the more complex particles they form.

Read this introduction to the types of primary collisions in the LHC, and to the Feynman Diagrams that physicists use to depict them. The references below provide some additional background on the particles colliding in the LHC and measured directly or indirectly by CMS.
