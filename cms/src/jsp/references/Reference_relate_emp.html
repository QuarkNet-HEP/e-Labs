Milestone: Relate energy, mass and momentum and explain the role of conservation of mass/energy, momentum and charge in analyzing CMS events.

Science builds on the application of well-established concepts to new areas of inquiry.

Conservation of Energy/Mass and of Momentum are fundamental concepts in physics. In high energy physics, the near-light speeds of colliding and emerging particles bring Relativity into the picture as well. At CMS, these fundamental physics concepts are involved in event analysis.

Learn how mass, energy and momentum apply in the collisions CMS detects by reading this explanation.

Answer the following questions in your logbook:

What can we know about the total energy and momentum of the particles that actually collide in the LHC? Why is transverse momentum central in the analysis of CMS events? Define the concepts of rest mass, kinetic energy, and the total energy of a particle. What are the relative sizes of each of these three quantities for the particles colliding in the LHC?
