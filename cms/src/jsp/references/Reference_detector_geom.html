Milestone: Summarize the geometry and function of the CMS detector.

Scientists design and deploy instruments guided by the questions they are asking, available technologies and cost of materials available to them.

The CMS Collaboration has built a complex detector to advance its discovery mission. To participate in their investigations, you need to grasp the basic structure and function of the CMS detector and its sub-detectors.

Start with this interactive graphic; get to know the sub-detectors, their order and their roles.

Answer the following questions in your logbook:

  • Discuss how the measurements made by CMS affect the size and shape of the detector.
  • Differentiate amongst the following CMS sub-detectors in terms of function and location of each: trackers, calorimeters, muon chambers, magnet system.
