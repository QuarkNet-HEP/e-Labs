Help on Determining the Most Likely Parent

Muon pairs, also called dimuons, can result from the decay of a Z, J/Psi and Upsilon particle as well as other processes. We can say an event with dimuons with an invariant mass close to the invariant mass of a particular particle is more likely to be the parent particle. If the invariant mass of the dimuons is not close to the invariant mass of the Z, J/Psi or Upsilon, then it probably does not have those particles as their parent particle. We cannot necessarily know for sure what the parent particle is, but by accumulating enough events, we will begin to see patterns of peaks at the masses of these three particles.
